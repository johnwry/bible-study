# Chapter 17

## The Passage They Couldn’t Ignore

The next evening they met again.

Same place.

Same table.

Adrián was already there.

Of course.

He was leaning over the counter talking with the girl behind the register as if they had known each other for years.

Matías walked in first.

He nodded toward the counter.

“Already making friends?”

Adrián grinned.

“Someone has to keep this place alive.”

Matías sat down quietly.

A moment later Elías arrived, carrying a Bible that already looked heavily marked.

Marcos came in last.

Adrián finally left the counter and joined them.

“Well,” he said.

“Did anyone do their homework?”

Marcos smiled.

“You make it sound like a school assignment.”

“It feels like one,” Adrián replied.

Elías opened his Bible immediately.

“I think we should start with Galatians.”

Matías looked up.

“Why Galatians?”

Elías answered simply.

“Because Paul says something there that is impossible to ignore.”

Adrián leaned forward.

“Now you have my attention.”

Elías turned a few pages and began reading.

> “You foolish Galatians, who has bewitched you?”

Adrián raised an eyebrow.

“That’s not a gentle opening.”

“No,” Marcos said.

“It isn’t.”

Elías continued.

> “Before whose eyes Jesus Christ was publicly portrayed as crucified.”

Matías nodded.

“They had heard the gospel clearly.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

Then he read the next line.

> “This is the only thing I want to find out from you…”

Adrián smiled slightly.

“That sounds like a very direct question.”

“Yes.”

Elías read it slowly.

> “Did you receive the Spirit by the works of the Law, or by hearing with faith?”

The table was quiet.

Matías answered first.

“By faith.”

“Of course.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“But listen to what Paul says next.”

He read again.

> “Are you so foolish?”

Adrián laughed softly.

“Paul is not being subtle.”

Elías continued.

> “Having begun by the Spirit…”

He paused.

“…are you now being perfected by the flesh?”

The sentence hung in the air.

No one spoke.

Adrián finally said what everyone was thinking.

“That’s strange.”

Matías frowned.

“How so?”

Adrián gestured toward the text.

“They began correctly.”

“Yes.”

“By the Spirit.”

“Yes.”

“But Paul says they were now trying to continue…”

He tapped the verse lightly.

“…by the flesh.”

Matías leaned forward.

“That sounds like effort.”

Marcos nodded.

“Yes.”

Religious effort.

The table grew quiet again.

Adrián spoke slowly.

“So let me understand this.”

“Galatia wasn’t immoral.”

“No,” Marcos said.

“They were trying to obey God.”

“Yes.”

“They were serious about holiness.”

“Yes.”

Adrián looked back at the verse.

“And Paul still calls that flesh.”

No one answered.

Because the implication was becoming obvious.

Matías spoke quietly.

“That’s difficult to accept.”

“Why,” Adrián asked.

Matías hesitated.

“Because effort is how we usually fight sin.”

Marcos nodded.

“Yes.”

“But according to Paul…”

He pointed to the passage.

“…that effort itself can become the flesh.”

Elías spoke carefully.

“Paul isn’t attacking obedience.”

“No.”

“He’s confronting the source of it.”

Adrián frowned slightly.

“What do you mean?”

Elías answered slowly.

“They began by depending on the Spirit.”

“Yes.”

“But now they were depending on themselves.”

The words settled heavily over the table.

Matías leaned back.

“That’s unsettling.”

“Why,” Marcos asked.

“Because it means the flesh can appear…”

He searched for the right words.

“…very religious.”

Adrián laughed quietly.

“That might explain Galatia.”

“Yes,” Marcos said.

“But it might explain something else too.”

“What,” Adrián asked.

Marcos looked around the table.

“It might explain Ephesus.”

Elías looked down at the passage again.

“And possibly Laodicea.”

Adrián leaned back slowly.

“So the flesh isn’t just sinful behavior.”

“No.”

“And it isn’t simply lack of discipline.”

“No.”

“And it isn’t only doctrinal confusion.”

“No.”

Matías spoke quietly.

“It might actually be something far more common.”

“What,” Adrián asked.

Matías answered carefully.

“Trying to live the Christian life by our own strength.”

No one spoke.

Because every one of them knew that described something painfully familiar.

Adrián finally said what none of them were ready to say out loud.

“If that’s true…”

He looked at the others.

“…then carnality might be much closer to us than we think.”

The table fell silent again.

Because the investigation had just crossed a line.

They were no longer studying distant churches.

They were beginning to study themselves.

------

This chapter delivers the **first real crack in the system**.

The discovery:

Carnality is not merely **sinful behavior**.

It can also be **religious effort carried out independently of the Spirit**.

Which is exactly what **Galatians 3:3** exposes.

And now the investigation deepens.
