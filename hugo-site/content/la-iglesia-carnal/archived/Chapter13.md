# Chapter 13

## The Correct Church

The conversation slowed again.

Three very different pictures of church life were now sitting on the table.

A lively church.

A serious church.

And a letter from Paul describing a divided church full of gifts.

Adrián finally looked at Elías.

“You’ve been very quiet.”

Elías smiled faintly.

“I’ve been listening.”

“That’s suspicious.”

Marcos laughed softly.

“Why.”

“Because when Elías gets quiet it usually means he’s thinking about a passage somewhere.”

Elías didn’t deny it.

“Actually,” he said, “there is one.”

Adrián leaned forward.

“Of course there is.”

“Where.”

“Revelation.”

Matías looked up.

“The letters to the churches?”

“Yes.”

Marcos nodded slowly.

“That could be helpful.”

Elías opened the Bible again and turned a few pages.

“This one has always interested me.”

“Which one?” Adrián asked.

“El mensaje a la iglesia en Éfeso.”

Matías leaned closer.

“That was a strong church.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“Very strong.”

Adrián smiled.

“Well that’s refreshing.”

“Why.”

“Because so far every church we’ve looked at has been a mess.”

Elías began reading.

> “I know your deeds and your toil and perseverance…”

Matías nodded.

“That’s good.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

He continued.

> “That you cannot tolerate evil men…”

Adrián leaned forward slightly.

“That’s also good.”

“Yes.”

Elías kept reading.

> “You put to the test those who call themselves apostles, and they are not…”

Marcos nodded.

“They tested doctrine.”

“Yes.”

Elías read again.

> “You have perseverance and have endured for My name’s sake…”

Adrián sat back.

“Now that sounds like a solid church.”

Matías nodded slowly.

“Yes.”

“They were careful.”

“Very careful.”

Elías continued reading.

> “And you have not grown weary.”

Marcos looked impressed.

“That’s remarkable.”

“Yes,” Elías said quietly.

The others waited.

Because they knew the passage wasn’t finished.

Elías read the next line slowly.

> “But I have this against you…”

The words settled over the table.

Adrián frowned slightly.

“That changed quickly.”

Matías leaned forward.

“What was the problem?”

Elías read again.

> “You have left your first love.”

No one spoke.

Adrián finally said,

“That’s… surprising.”

Matías nodded slowly.

“With everything else they were doing right.”

“Yes.”

Marcos leaned back in his chair.

“So the church was doctrinally careful.”

“Yes.”

“Morally serious.”

“Yes.”

“Disciplined.”

“Yes.”

“And yet Christ still rebukes them.”

“Yes.”

Adrián rubbed his chin.

“So let me understand something.”

“What,” Marcos said.

“Correct doctrine didn’t prevent the problem.”

“No,” Elías said quietly.

Matías spoke slowly.

“Doctrine is still essential.”

“Of course,” Elías replied.

“No one is questioning that.”

Marcos nodded.

“But this passage shows something important.”

“What.”

“Correct doctrine alone does not guarantee spiritual life.”

Adrián leaned back.

“That’s uncomfortable.”

“Why,” Matías asked.

“Because a lot of churches measure health by how correct their teaching is.”

“Yes,” Marcos said.

“But Ephesus had correct teaching.”

Matías looked at the passage again.

“They even tested false apostles.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“They were careful.”

“And yet something had still gone wrong.”

Adrián looked thoughtful.

“What exactly does it mean that they left their first love?”

Elías answered slowly.

“It means their devotion to Christ had grown cold.”

“But their doctrine hadn’t,” Marcos said.

“Exactly.”

Matías sat quietly.

Finally he said,

“That’s subtle.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“Very subtle.”

Adrián leaned forward again.

“So a church can…”

He paused.

“…teach the truth.”

“Yes.”

“Defend the truth.”

“Yes.”

“Protect the truth.”

“Yes.”

“And still lose something essential.”

The others nodded.

Marcos spoke quietly.

“That might be the most dangerous situation we’ve seen yet.”

“Why,” Adrián asked.

“Because everything still looks right.”

Elías closed the Bible slowly.

“Yes.”

“That’s exactly why Christ warned them.”

Matías spoke quietly.

“What did He tell them to do?”

Elías answered.

“Remember where they had fallen from.”

“And return.”

The table sat quietly again.

After a moment Adrián said,

“So now we’ve seen three different kinds of churches.”

“What do you mean,” Marcos asked.

“A lively church like Corinth.”

“Yes.”

“A serious church like Galatia.”

“Yes.”

“And a correct church like Ephesus.”

Matías added quietly,

“And each of them still had a problem.”

Adrián nodded slowly.

“Yes.”

“And that makes me wonder something.”

“What,” Elías asked.

Adrián looked around the table.

“What would a church look like…”

He paused.

“…that Christ actually approves?”

No one answered.

Because the investigation had now revealed something none of them had expected.

Carnality could appear in excitement.

Carnality could appear in religious effort.

And apparently…

Carnality could even appear in doctrinal correctness.

And if that was true…

Then recognizing a truly spiritual church might be far harder than they had ever imagined.
