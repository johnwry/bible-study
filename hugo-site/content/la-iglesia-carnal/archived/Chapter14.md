# Chapter 14

## The Successful Church

For a while the table was quiet.

Not uncomfortable.

Just thoughtful.

They had now looked at three churches.

Three very different kinds of churches.

And yet all three had received correction in Scripture.

Adrián finally broke the silence.

“Well,” he said, “if we’re being fair…”

He looked across the table.

“…we should probably look at the last one.”

Marcos nodded slowly.

“You mean Laodicea.”

“Yes.”

Matías leaned back slightly.

“That one is easier.”

Adrián smiled.

“That’s what everyone says.”

Marcos opened the Bible and found the passage.

Revelation 3.

He read aloud.

> “To the angel of the church in Laodicea write…”

Adrián leaned forward.

“Let’s hear it.”

Marcos continued.

> “I know your deeds…”

Matías nodded.

“So they were active.”

“Yes.”

Marcos read the next line.

> “That you are neither cold nor hot…”

Adrián frowned slightly.

“That’s the famous part.”

“Yes.”

Marcos continued.

> “Because you are lukewarm, and neither hot nor cold, I will spit you out of My mouth.”

Adrián sat back.

“That’s strong.”

“Yes,” Elías said quietly.

Matías spoke slowly.

“I’ve always heard that explained as spiritual apathy.”

“Yes,” Marcos said.

“That’s the usual explanation.”

Adrián rubbed his chin.

“But the passage keeps going.”

Marcos nodded and continued reading.

> “Because you say, ‘I am rich, and have become wealthy, and have need of nothing.’”

Adrián laughed softly.

“That sounds confident.”

“Yes.”

Marcos finished the sentence.

> “And you do not know that you are wretched and miserable and poor and blind and naked.”

The words settled over the table.

Adrián finally said,

“That’s quite a difference.”

Matías nodded.

“They thought they were doing well.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“But Christ said the opposite.”

Marcos closed the Bible slightly.

“What interests me is what they believed about themselves.”

“What do you mean,” Adrián asked.

“They said they needed nothing.”

Matías thought about that.

“That suggests success.”

“Yes.”

Adrián leaned forward.

“Successful churches are usually admired.”

Marcos nodded.

“Large attendance.”

“Many programs.”

“Active ministries.”

Matías added,

“Financial stability.”

Elías spoke quietly.

“And influence.”

Adrián smiled.

“That sounds like the kind of church everyone wants.”

“Yes,” Marcos said.

“But apparently Christ saw something different.”

Matías asked the obvious question.

“What exactly was the problem?”

Marcos looked back at the passage.

“They believed they were spiritually rich.”

“But they weren’t,” Adrián said.

“Exactly.”

Elías spoke slowly.

“That may be the most dangerous condition yet.”

Adrián tilted his head.

“Why.”

“Because they were completely unaware.”

Matías nodded.

“They didn’t feel broken.”

“They felt successful.”

Adrián thought about that.

“So Corinth had visible problems.”

“Yes.”

“Galatia had doctrinal confusion.”

“Yes.”

“Ephesus had lost love.”

“Yes.”

“But Laodicea…”

He paused.

“…didn’t even know anything was wrong.”

Marcos nodded.

“Yes.”

Elías spoke carefully.

“That may be why Christ spoke so directly to them.”

Adrián looked at the passage again.

“What did He tell them to do?”

Marcos answered.

“He told them to buy gold refined by fire.”

“White garments.”

“And eye salve.”

Matías nodded slowly.

“So they needed something they didn’t realize they lacked.”

“Yes.”

Adrián leaned back.

“That’s unsettling.”

“Why,” Elías asked.

“Because it means a church can look strong…”

He paused.

“…and still be spiritually empty.”

The table sat quietly again.

Finally Matías spoke.

“So now we’ve seen four.”

Adrián nodded.

“Yes.”

“Corinth.”

“Galatia.”

“Ephesus.”

“Laodicea.”

Marcos spoke slowly.

“And all four had something in common.”

Adrián raised an eyebrow.

“What.”

Marcos looked around the table.

“Christ rebuked them.”

Elías nodded.

“Yes.”

Matías looked thoughtful.

“That means something important.”

“What,” Adrián asked.

“That carnality…”

He paused.

“…does not always look the same.”

Adrián nodded slowly.

“No.”

“Sometimes it looks exciting.”

“Sometimes it looks serious.”

“Sometimes it looks correct.”

“And sometimes…”

He gestured slightly.

“…it looks successful.”

The table went quiet again.

Because something had now become unavoidable.

Carnality could wear many different masks.

And if that was true…

Recognizing it might be far harder than anyone expected.

Adrián finally said what all of them were thinking.

“So let me ask the obvious question.”

The others looked at him.

“If all four of these churches were carnal in different ways…”

He paused.

“…what exactly **is** carnality?”

No one answered immediately.

Because that question was about to change the entire direction of their investigation.
