# Chapter 18

## The Passage That Made Them Uncomfortable

The table stayed quiet longer than usual.

No one rushed to speak.

Galatians had already unsettled them.

Adrián finally broke the silence.

“Well.”

He leaned back slightly.

“That was… unpleasant.”

Matías nodded slowly.

“Yes.”

Adrián looked at him.

“You don’t like the idea that effort can be flesh.”

Matías didn’t answer immediately.

“No,” he said finally.

“I don’t.”

Marcos looked down at his Bible.

“There’s another passage.”

Adrián sighed.

“That doesn’t sound encouraging.”

Marcos smiled faintly.

“It probably won’t be.”

Elías leaned forward.

“Where?”

“Romans.”

Adrián laughed softly.

“That’s never a light conversation.”

Marcos turned to the passage.

“Romans chapter eight.”

Matías nodded.

“That’s a strong chapter.”

“Yes.”

Marcos read quietly.

> “For those who are according to the flesh set their minds on the things of the flesh…”

He paused.

“…but those who are according to the Spirit, the things of the Spirit.”

Adrián rubbed his chin.

“That sounds like two directions.”

“Yes,” Marcos said.

“Two completely different orientations.”

Matías nodded.

“Flesh versus Spirit.”

Marcos continued reading.

> “For the mind set on the flesh is death…”

Adrián frowned.

“That escalated quickly.”

Marcos finished the sentence.

> “…but the mind set on the Spirit is life and peace.”

The table was quiet again.

Elías spoke carefully.

“That doesn’t sound like a small difference.”

“No,” Marcos said.

“It sounds absolute.”

Matías leaned forward slightly.

“Read the next verse.”

Marcos did.

> “Because the mind set on the flesh is hostile toward God…”

Adrián raised an eyebrow.

“Hostile?”

“Yes.”

Marcos continued.

> “…for it does not subject itself to the law of God, for it is not even able to do so.”

Adrián looked puzzled.

“Wait.”

“That doesn’t sound right.”

Matías looked at him.

“What do you mean?”

Adrián gestured toward the text.

“That sounds like rebellion.”

“Yes,” Marcos said.

“But Galatia wasn’t rebelling.”

“They were trying to obey.”

Matías nodded slowly.

“That’s true.”

Adrián leaned forward.

“So how can Paul call something hostile to God…”

He tapped the page.

“…when the people involved were trying to do the right thing?”

No one answered immediately.

Because that question was the heart of the problem.

Marcos read the next verse.

> “And those who are in the flesh cannot please God.”

The words landed heavily.

Adrián blinked.

“Cannot?”

“Yes.”

Matías frowned deeply.

“That sounds absolute.”

Elías nodded slowly.

“It is.”

Adrián sat back.

“That’s disturbing.”

“Why?” Marcos asked.

Adrián shrugged.

“Because most religious effort is aimed at pleasing God.”

“Yes,” Marcos said.

“But Paul says something important.”

“What,” Matías asked.

“That effort, if it comes from the flesh…”

He pointed to the verse.

“…cannot please Him.”

The table went quiet again.

Matías finally spoke.

“So now we have two statements.”

Adrián nodded.

“From Galatians.”

“Yes.”

“And from Romans.”

Matías repeated them slowly.

“Beginning by the Spirit…”

“…but trying to continue by the flesh.”

“And…”

He looked down at the page.

“…those who are in the flesh cannot please God.”

Adrián leaned back.

“That creates a serious problem.”

“What problem?” Elías asked.

Adrián looked around the table.

“If the flesh cannot please God…”

He paused.

“…then religious effort alone doesn’t solve carnality.”

Matías looked down at his hands.

“That’s difficult to accept.”

Marcos spoke quietly.

“But it explains something.”

“What?” Adrián asked.

“Why churches can appear very serious…”

“…very disciplined…”

“…very correct…”

“…and still remain carnal.”

The words hung in the air.

Elías finally spoke.

“So the real question becomes…”

He looked at the others.

“…what exactly produces the Christian life?”

Adrián nodded slowly.

“Yes.”

“Because if it isn’t human effort…”

“…then something else must be happening.”

Matías looked back at the passage.

“The Spirit.”

“Yes,” Marcos said quietly.

“The Spirit.”

Adrián exhaled slowly.

“Well.”

He looked around the table.

“I think this investigation just became a lot more personal.”

No one disagreed.

Because every one of them had just realized something unsettling.

They had spent years trying to fight the flesh.

But they had never seriously considered the possibility that the struggle itself…

might sometimes be coming from the flesh.

Matías looked up immediately.

“Wait.”

The others turned toward him.

“What do you mean *sometimes*?”

Adrián shrugged slightly.

“Well… you know.”

“Sometimes we try too hard.”

Matías shook his head.

“No.”

“That’s not what Paul said.”

The table went quiet again.

Elías looked down at the passage.

“He’s right.”

Adrián frowned.

“What do you mean?”

Elías tapped the verse gently.

“Paul didn’t say the flesh sometimes fails to please God.”

He read the line again slowly.

> “Those who are in the flesh **cannot** please God.”

Adrián leaned forward again.

“So?”

“So the problem isn’t that the flesh fails occasionally.”

Elías looked up.

“The problem is that the flesh **cannot produce the Christian life at all**.”

The words hung in the air.

Marcos spoke quietly.

“That would mean something important.”

“What?” Adrián asked.

“It would mean that trying harder…”

He paused.

“…doesn’t actually solve carnality.”

Matías finished the thought.

“It might actually be part of it.”

No one spoke for a moment.

Because that possibility was far more unsettling than anything they had discussed so far.

Adrián finally said what everyone was thinking.

“Well…”

He leaned back slowly.

“That changes the whole investigation.”
