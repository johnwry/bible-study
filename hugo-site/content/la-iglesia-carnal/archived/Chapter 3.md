# Chapter 3

## What Is the Flesh?

For a few seconds no one spoke.

The café hummed quietly around them. Cups clinked somewhere behind the counter. Someone near the door laughed too loudly at something on their phone.

But at the table by the window the mood had shifted.

Adrián finally broke the silence.

“Well that’s awkward.”

Marcos looked at him.

“What.”

“We just discovered we’ve all been using a word we apparently don’t understand.”

Matías leaned back slightly.

“I wouldn’t say we don’t understand it.”

Adrián smiled.

“Alright then.”

He gestured toward Matías.

“You go first.”

Matías hesitated.

“Define the flesh.”

Matías looked at the table for a moment before answering.

“The flesh is our sinful nature.”

“That’s what most people say,” Marcos said.

“Yes.”

“And?”

Matías shrugged slightly.

“It’s the part of us that wants to disobey God.”

Adrián nodded.

“That’s how I’ve heard it explained too.”

Elías had been quiet.

He spoke slowly.

“But that doesn’t quite fit what we just read.”

Matías looked at him.

“Why not?”

Elías tapped the open Bible gently.

“Paul didn’t mention immoral behavior.”

“That doesn’t mean it isn’t included.”

“No,” Elías said. “But that wasn’t the example he used.”

Marcos leaned forward slightly.

“He used jealousy.”

“And strife,” Adrián added.

“Yes,” Elías said.

“That’s relational conflict.”

Matías folded his arms.

“That’s still sin.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“But it’s not the kind of sin most people think about when they hear the word flesh.”

Adrián laughed quietly.

“That’s true.”

“What do people usually think of?” Marcos asked.

Adrián raised his eyebrows.

“Oh you know.”

“What.”

“The obvious stuff.”

Matías shook his head slightly.

“That’s part of the flesh.”

“But apparently not the only part,” Elías said.

Marcos looked thoughtful.

“So maybe the flesh is bigger than we think.”

Adrián pointed at him.

“That sounds dangerous.”

“Why.”

“Because if the definition gets bigger…”

He leaned back in his chair.

“More people get included.”

Marcos smiled.

“That might be the problem.”

Matías wasn’t smiling.

“The flesh is serious.”

“No one said it wasn’t,” Adrián said.

“But how do you define it?”

Matías thought for a moment.

“Human effort.”

The others looked at him.

“Explain,” Marcos said.

Matías spoke carefully.

“The flesh is when a person tries to live the Christian life by their own strength instead of depending on God.”

Elías nodded slowly.

“That’s closer.”

Adrián tilted his head.

“But hold on.”

“What.”

“If that’s true…”

He gestured toward the Bible.

“Then those people in Corinth weren’t trying too hard.”

Marcos laughed quietly.

“That’s true.”

Matías didn’t answer.

Elías spoke again.

“There might be something else here.”

“What.”

“The flesh might not just be immoral behavior.”

Marcos nodded.

“And it might not just be effort either.”

Adrián looked at him.

“Well that narrows it down.”

Marcos ignored him.

“Maybe the flesh is simply human life operating independently from God.”

Elías looked at him.

“That would explain a lot.”

Matías frowned slightly.

“But if that’s the case…”

He paused.

“Then the flesh could appear very different depending on the situation.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“In one place it might look like sin.”

“And in another place,” Marcos said,

“It might look like religion.”

Adrián leaned forward slowly.

“Now that’s interesting.”

The table went quiet again.

After a moment Adrián spoke.

“Let me tell you how I’ve always thought about it.”

The others waited.

“I figured the flesh meant the obvious stuff.”

“What obvious stuff?” Marcos asked.

“You know.”

Adrián waved his hand vaguely.

“Drinking too much. Sleeping around. That kind of thing.”

Matías nodded.

“That’s part of it.”

“Right.”

Adrián shrugged.

“So as long as I wasn’t doing those things…”

He grinned.

“I figured I was doing alright.”

Marcos smiled.

“That’s probably how most people think.”

“Yes,” Elías said quietly.

“But that doesn’t seem to be Paul’s definition.”

Matías leaned forward again.

“Then what is?”

Nobody answered immediately.

Finally Marcos said,

“If we’re serious about this…”

He pointed toward the Bible.

“Then maybe we should find out how Scripture uses the word.”

Adrián nodded slowly.

“I like that.”

Elías agreed.

“Yes.”

Matías looked thoughtful.

“How would we do that?”

Marcos shrugged.

“We could trace it.”

“Trace it?”

“Follow the word.”

“Where it appears,” Elías said.

“Yes.”

“And see what Scripture says about it.”

Adrián smiled.

“Well now.”

He lifted his cup again.

“This investigation is getting interesting.”

Matías wasn’t smiling.

He was staring quietly at the open Bible.

Because something had begun to bother him.

If the flesh meant more than obvious sin…

Then avoiding obvious sin might not solve the problem at all.

And that thought was harder to ignore than he expected.
