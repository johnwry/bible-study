# Chapter 2

## Why Corinth?

The coffee had cooled a little.

Adrián turned the cup slowly in his hands while the others sat quietly for a moment.

Finally he looked up.

“So… Corinth.”

Elías nodded.

“I think so.”

“You’re not sure?”

“I’m fairly sure,” Elías said. “But I’d want to see it.”

Marcos leaned back slightly.

“Why Corinth?”

Elías thought for a moment.

“I remember Paul saying something about believers being *carnal*.”

Matías frowned.

“That’s a strong word.”

“Yes.”

“Are you sure he was talking about believers?”

“That’s what I remember.”

Adrián sat up a little.

“Well now I’m curious.”

Marcos looked at Elías.

“Do you remember where?”

Elías shook his head.

“Not exactly.”

“That narrows it down.”

Adrián laughed.

“It’s in there somewhere.”

Matías looked thoughtful.

“If Paul actually called a church carnal…”

He paused.

“That would be serious.”

“Yes,” Elías said quietly.

Marcos stirred his coffee again.

“But before we go chasing a word…”

He looked at the others.

“Maybe we should ask something first.”

“What.”

“What do we mean by carnal?”

Adrián pointed at him.

“Good question.”

“Everyone uses the word.”

“Yes.”

“But what does it actually mean?”

Matías answered first.

“Fleshly.”

Adrián smiled.

“Well that clears everything up.”

Matías ignored him.

“I mean controlled by the flesh.”

Elías nodded slightly.

“That would make sense.”

Marcos looked around the table.

“But how would you know?”

No one answered immediately.

Adrián finally said,

“I’ve heard people say it means worldly.”

“Yes,” Marcos said.

“That’s common.”

Matías added,

“Living in sin.”

Elías shook his head slightly.

“That seems too simple.”

“Why.”

“Because Paul was writing to churches.”

“So?”

“So if that’s all he meant,” Elías said, “then every church that struggles with sin would be carnal.”

Adrián leaned forward.

“That might actually be true.”

Marcos laughed softly.

“That would make things interesting.”

Matías looked uneasy.

“I doubt Paul meant it that loosely.”

“What do you think he meant?” Adrián asked.

Matías took a moment before answering.

“I think he meant believers who are not spiritually mature.”

Elías nodded slowly.

“That could be closer.”

Marcos looked back at Elías.

“But you said something else.”

“What.”

“That Paul actually used the word.”

“Yes.”

“Then maybe we should see what he meant instead of guessing.”

Adrián lifted his hands.

“I like that plan.”

“Of course you do.”

“Why wouldn't I?”

“Because it might prove you wrong.”

Adrián grinned.

“That has never stopped me before.”

Matías leaned forward slightly.

“If we’re going to do that…”

He paused.

“Then we should actually read the passage.”

Elías nodded.

“Yes.”

Adrián looked around the table.

“Anyone have a Bible?”

Three hands went to their bags almost at the same time.

Marcos laughed.

“Well that was predictable.”

Elías opened his Bible and flipped slowly through the pages.

“Let’s see.”

The others waited.

The café noise hummed quietly around them.

Finally Elías stopped.

“I found it.”

“Where?” Adrián asked.

“First Corinthians.”

He turned the Bible so the others could see.

“Chapter three.”

Matías leaned closer.

“What does it say?”

Elías read slowly.

> “And I, brothers, could not speak to you as to spiritual men, but as to men of flesh, as to infants in Christ.”

Adrián sat back slightly.

“Wait.”

He pointed.

“Did he say *brothers*?”

“Yes.”

“So believers.”

“Yes.”

“But then he calls them fleshly.”

Matías looked at the page carefully.

“Keep reading.”

Elías continued.

> “I gave you milk to drink, not solid food; for you were not yet able to receive it.”

Marcos nodded slowly.

“Immaturity.”

“Apparently.”

Elías kept reading.

> “Indeed, even now you are not yet able, for you are still fleshly.”

Adrián whistled quietly.

“He said it twice.”

“Yes.”

Matías leaned closer.

“What comes next?”

Elías read again.

> “For since there is jealousy and strife among you, are you not fleshly?”

Nobody spoke for a moment.

Finally Marcos said,

“So he tells us how he knows.”

“How.”

Marcos pointed to the page.

“Jealousy.”

“And strife,” Adrián added.

Matías frowned slightly.

“That seems… relational.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“Not immoral behavior exactly.”

“Not exactly,” Marcos said.

Adrián leaned back in his chair.

“So let me get this straight.”

He pointed toward the Bible.

“Paul is talking to believers.”

“Yes.”

“And he calls them fleshly.”

“Yes.”

“And the evidence is…”

He paused.

“Jealousy and strife.”

Elías nodded.

“That’s what it says.”

Matías sat quietly for a moment.

Finally he said,

“That’s… interesting.”

Adrián laughed.

“That’s one way to say it.”

Marcos tapped the table gently.

“If that’s the case…”

He looked at the others.

“Then carnality might not mean what we think it means.”

Elías closed the Bible slowly.

“Yes.”

“That’s what I’m starting to wonder.”

Adrián leaned forward again.

“Well now I have another question.”

“What.”

He looked around the table.

“What exactly is the *flesh*?”

This time no one answered.

And that bothered them more than they expected.
