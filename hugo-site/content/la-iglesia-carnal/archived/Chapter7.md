# Chapter 7

## By Their Fruit

The list was still sitting in front of them.

Jealousy.

Strife.

Factions.

The words felt heavier the longer they looked at them.

Adrián finally pushed his chair back a little.

“Well.”

He rubbed his face.

“That was cheerful.”

Marcos smiled faintly.

“It was honest.”

Matías was still staring at the passage.

“What bothers me,” he said slowly, “is how ordinary these things are.”

“What do you mean?” Adrián asked.

“They’re not shocking.”

“No,” Elías said.

“They’re common.”

Marcos nodded.

“Very common.”

Adrián leaned forward again.

“Which raises another problem.”

“What problem?” Marcos asked.

“If these things are works of the flesh…”

He tapped the page lightly.

“Then they probably appear in more churches than we’d like to admit.”

Matías frowned slightly.

“That doesn’t mean the whole church is carnal.”

“No,” Elías said.

“But it might reveal something about its condition.”

Marcos looked thoughtful.

“You know what this reminds me of?”

“What,” Adrián said.

“Something Jesus said.”

Elías nodded slowly.

“I was thinking the same thing.”

Adrián looked between them.

“Which thing.”

Marcos answered.

“You know a tree by its fruit.”

Adrián sat back.

“Oh.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“If the fruit appears…”

He paused.

“It tells you something about the tree.”

Matías leaned back slightly.

“That’s Matthew 7.”

“Yes,” Marcos said.

“Jesus said good trees produce good fruit.”

“And bad trees produce bad fruit,” Adrián finished.

Elías nodded.

“Yes.”

Adrián rubbed his chin.

“So if jealousy, strife, and factions are works of the flesh…”

He looked at the others.

“Then they are fruit.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“Fruit of the flesh.”

Marcos added,

“And fruit reveals the source.”

Matías looked thoughtful.

“So if those things appear repeatedly…”

He paused.

“They reveal what is feeding the church.”

Adrián laughed softly.

“That’s uncomfortable.”

“Why,” Marcos asked.

“Because most churches would never say they operate in the flesh.”

“No,” Elías said.

“But the fruit might say otherwise.”

Matías spoke slowly.

“There’s something else about fruit.”

“What,” Adrián asked.

“It doesn’t appear overnight.”

“No,” Elías said.

“It grows over time.”

Marcos nodded.

“Yes.”

“Fruit is the result of something being cultivated.”

The four men sat quietly for a moment.

Adrián finally spoke.

“So let me see if I understand this.”

He pointed toward the Bible.

“If we want to know whether a church is operating in the flesh…”

He paused.

“We should look at the fruit.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“What kind of relationships exist.”

“Yes.”

“How people treat each other.”

“Yes.”

Marcos nodded.

“And whether jealousy and factions appear.”

Adrián leaned back slowly.

“Well.”

He looked around the table.

“That would make diagnosing a church possible.”

Matías raised an eyebrow.

“Diagnosing?”

“Yes.”

“Like a doctor?”

“Exactly.”

Marcos smiled.

“That’s not a bad comparison.”

Elías nodded.

“Scripture describes the condition.”

“And the fruit reveals it,” Marcos added.

Matías looked down at the table.

“That’s serious.”

Adrián tilted his head.

“Why.”

Matías answered quietly.

“Because if we applied that principle honestly…”

He paused.

“We might discover things we don’t want to see.”

No one answered.

Because they were all thinking the same thing.

Adrián finally broke the silence.

“Well.”

He lifted his cup again.

“If we’re going to do this…”

He looked around the table.

“We might as well do it properly.”

Marcos nodded.

“How.”

Adrián smiled.

“Let’s start paying attention.”

“To what?” Elías asked.

“The fruit.”

The word hung in the air.

Because if fruit reveals the source…

Then the condition of a church might not be hidden nearly as well as people imagine.
