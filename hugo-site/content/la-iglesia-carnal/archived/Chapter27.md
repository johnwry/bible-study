# Chapter 27

## The Cross Protects From Carnality

That evening the café was quieter than usual.

Not because fewer people were there.

But because the conversation at the table had slowed down.

For days the four men had been pressing into the same question.

Carnality.

They had seen it in Corinth.

They had seen it in Galatia.

They had seen it hidden behind discipline in Ephesus.

They had seen it thriving under activity in Laodicea.

And now the cross had entered the conversation.

Adrián broke the silence.

“So if the cross confronts carnality…”

He paused.

“And if the cross disarms it…”

He looked at the others.

“What does that mean for daily life?”

Matías nodded slowly.

“That’s the real question.”

Marcos flipped a few pages in his Bible.

“There’s a verse that might answer that.”

Adrián smiled.

“Another one?”

Marcos nodded.

“This one is simple.”

He read it slowly.

> “But I say, walk by the Spirit, and you will not carry out the desire of the flesh.”

Adrián looked up.

“That sounds almost too simple.”

“Yes,” Matías said.

“But listen carefully to how it works.”

Elías leaned forward.

“The verse does not say…”

He paused.

“…fight the flesh.”

Marcos nodded.

“It also does not say…”

“…focus on the flesh.”

Adrián looked down at the verse again.

“You’re right.”

“It says walk by the Spirit.”

Matías spoke slowly.

“That means the protection is not found in resisting the flesh.”

The others looked at him.

“It is found in walking somewhere else.”

Adrián leaned back in his chair.

“That’s very different from how most people describe the Christian life.”

“How so?” Marcos asked.

“Well,” Adrián said,

“I’ve heard a lot of sermons about guarding yourself from the flesh.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“Be careful.”

“Be alert.”

“Be on your guard.”

Matías nodded.

“Constant vigilance.”

Adrián smiled faintly.

“But Paul doesn’t say that here.”

“No,” Marcos said.

“He redirects the focus.”

Elías read the verse again quietly.

> “Walk by the Spirit…”

Adrián tapped the table lightly.

“That word walk is interesting.”

Matías nodded.

“Yes.”

“It suggests movement.”

“Daily life.”

“Ordinary steps.”

Marcos added,

“Not a battle posture.”

The table grew quiet.

Adrián finally spoke.

“So the cross protects us by redirecting our attention.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“From the flesh…”

“…to the Spirit.”

Matías thought about that.

“That would explain something.”

“What?” Adrián asked.

“Why the more people focus on the flesh…”

“…the more powerful it appears.”

Marcos nodded.

“Yes.”

“That’s often what happens.”

Adrián laughed softly.

“I’ve seen entire churches built around that idea.”

Matías smiled slightly.

“Carnality management.”

“Yes,” Adrián said.

“Endless strategies.”

“Endless warnings.”

“Endless pressure.”

Elías spoke quietly.

“But Paul’s instruction is surprisingly simple.”

Adrián looked at the verse again.

“Walk by the Spirit.”

Marcos nodded.

“And the result is stated clearly.”

Adrián finished the sentence.

“You will not carry out the desire of the flesh.”

The words hung in the air.

Matías spoke carefully.

“That’s not presented as a possibility.”

“No,” Marcos said.

“It’s presented as a certainty.”

Elías leaned back slightly.

“So the cross removes the authority of the flesh…”

“And the Spirit leads us away from it.”

Adrián nodded slowly.

“That sounds less like constant struggle…”

“…and more like protection.”

Matías smiled faintly.

“Yes.”

“Protection from something that has already been judged.”

The café noise filled the silence again.

Adrián spoke softly.

“That’s beginning to feel different.”

“How?” Marcos asked.

“For the first time in this conversation…”

Adrián paused.

“…the flesh doesn’t feel like the center of everything.”

The others nodded.

Matías spoke quietly.

“And maybe that’s exactly the point.”

The table grew still again.

Adrián looked around at the others.

“So if the cross confronts carnality…”

“…and disarms it…”

“…and protects us from it…”

He paused.

“That leaves one final question.”

“What question?” Elías asked.

Adrián smiled slightly.

“If the cross has done all this…”

“…why do so many churches still spend their energy managing the flesh?”

No one answered immediately.

Because that question was about to lead them somewhere deeper.
