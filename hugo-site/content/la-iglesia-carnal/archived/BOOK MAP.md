# BOOK MAP

## *La Iglesia Carnal* (working title)

------

# PART I — THE QUESTION

Purpose: introduce the men and the investigation.

### Chapter 1 — The Café Table

We meet:

- Adrián
- Elías
- Marcos
- Matías

Natural conversation.

Topic slowly moves toward church.

Someone asks:

**“What actually is a carnal church?”**

No one answers.

------

### Chapter 2 — Four Churches

Each man describes a church he visited while traveling.

They appear healthy.

The reader quietly recognizes types:

- Corinth
- Ephesus
- Laodicea
- Galatia

The men disagree on what they saw.

------

### Chapter 3 — Why Corinth?

Someone remembers:

> “Paul actually calls believers carnal.”

They decide to investigate.

They open **1 Corinthians**.

------

# PART II — THE DIAGNOSIS

Purpose: discover what Scripture says.

------

### Chapter 4 — Carnal Believers

Passage investigated:

**1 Corinthians 3:1–4**

Observations:

- Paul calls them **brothers**
- yet **carnal**
- jealousy and strife reveal it

Question arises:

**What exactly is the flesh?**

------

### Chapter 5 — What Is the Flesh?

Each man gives his definition.

All definitions reveal their assumptions.

They realize they **may not understand the word at all**.

------

### Chapter 6 — A Religious Flesh

Passage investigated:

**Galatians 3:1–5**

Shock:

The flesh can appear as **religious effort**.

Matías begins to struggle.

------

### Chapter 7 — The Works of the Flesh

Passage investigated:

**Galatians 5:16–21**

The flesh produces relational destruction:

- jealousy
- strife
- factions
- envy

This matches Corinth exactly.

------

### Chapter 8 — A Church That Is Correct

Passage investigated:

**Revelation 2 — Ephesus**

Elías becomes uneasy.

The church is:

- doctrinally correct
- disciplined

Yet Christ rebukes them.

------

### Chapter 9 — A Church That Is Successful

Passage investigated:

**Revelation 3 — Laodicea**

Marcos becomes uneasy.

The church is:

- active
- influential
- wealthy

Yet Christ says they are blind.

------

# PART III — THE PATTERN

Purpose: identify carnality clearly.

------

### Chapter 10 — By Their Fruit

Jesus’ principle applied.

Carnality produces visible results.

------

### Chapter 11 — What Carnality Always Produces

Carnal churches will always show:

- division
- control
- insecurity
- leadership protection
- fear of truth

------

### Chapter 12 — What Carnality Cannot Produce

Carnal churches cannot produce:

- freedom
- humility
- rest
- spiritual fruit

------

# PART IV — FAILED SOLUTIONS

Purpose: remove human answers.

------

### Chapter 13 — Trying Harder

Why discipline and effort cannot solve carnality.

------

### Chapter 14 — Carnality Management

Huge chapter.

Churches learn to:

- manage the flesh
- regulate behavior
- organize around carnality

Your key insight appears here:

> “If carnality disappeared tomorrow, what would our systems do?”

------

# PART V — THE DISCOVERY

Purpose: reveal the answer.

------

### Chapter 15 — Something We Missed

Return to **1 Corinthians 1**.

The cross was there from the beginning.

------

### Chapter 16 — The Word of the Cross

Text:

**1 Corinthians 1:18–31**

The cross destroys human boasting.

------

### Chapter 17 — The Cross Confronts Carnality

The cross exposes:

- wisdom
- status
- human strength
- effort

------

### Chapter 18 — The Cross Disarms Carnality

Dependence replaces effort.

------

# PART VI — LIFE

Purpose: show what real salvation produces.

------

### Chapter 19 — The Power of God Unto Salvation

Salvation produces life, not merely escape from carnality.

------

### Chapter 20 — Walking in the Spirit

Texts:

- Romans 6
- Romans 8
- Galatians 5

The Spirit produces fruit.

------

### Chapter 21 — Grace Has Made the Difference

The men reflect.

They realize something shocking:

**The solution had been present the whole time.**

------

# FINAL CHAPTER

### Chapter 22 — The Church That Lives

A church that:

- depends on Christ
- walks by the Spirit
- no longer organizes around carnality.
