# Chapter 6

## The Works of the Flesh

The Bible was still open on the table.

No one had moved it.

Adrián leaned forward again.

“So what else did you find?”

Elías turned a few pages slowly.

“There’s another passage that might help.”

“Where?”

“Same letter.”

“Galatians?”

“Yes.”

Marcos nodded.

“That makes sense.”

Elías stopped turning pages.

“Here.”

He looked down.

“Chapter five.”

Matías leaned closer.

“What does it say?”

Elías began reading.

> “Now the works of the flesh are evident…”

Adrián interrupted.

“Wait.”

“What.”

“That sounds helpful.”

Marcos smiled.

“Yes.”

“If the works of the flesh are evident,” Adrián said, “then maybe this will finally clear things up.”

Elías continued reading.

> “Immorality, impurity, sensuality…”

Matías nodded.

“That fits.”

“Yes,” Adrián said.

“That’s what we expected.”

Elías kept reading.

> “Idolatry, sorcery…”

Marcos shrugged.

“Also expected.”

Then Elías continued.

> “Enmities…”

He paused.

Marcos looked up.

“Enmities?”

“Yes.”

Adrián leaned forward.

“What does that mean?”

“Hostility,” Elías said.

“Conflict between people.”

Adrián sat back.

“That’s different.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

He continued reading.

> “Strife…”

Marcos nodded slowly.

“That word again.”

“Yes.”

“The same one we saw in Corinth.”

Elías nodded.

“Yes.”

Matías shifted slightly in his chair.

“Keep going.”

Elías read again.

> “Jealousy…”

Adrián pointed at the page.

“That one too.”

Marcos looked thoughtful.

“Interesting.”

Elías continued.

> “Outbursts of anger…”

Matías frowned.

“That certainly happens in churches.”

Adrián laughed.

“Understatement.”

Elías kept reading.

> “Disputes… dissensions… factions…”

The table went very quiet.

Marcos finally spoke.

“Factions.”

Adrián nodded slowly.

“That sounds familiar.”

Elías looked up from the page.

“Yes.”

Matías spoke quietly.

“That’s division.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“Groups forming against each other.”

Adrián leaned back.

“Well that’s uncomfortable.”

Marcos looked at the others.

“Look at the pattern.”

“What pattern?”

“These are relational fractures.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“People turning against each other.”

Matías was staring at the list.

“Jealousy.”

“Strife.”

“Factions.”

He shook his head slightly.

“That sounds exactly like Corinth.”

“Yes,” Adrián said.

“That’s what Paul said.”

Marcos tapped the table slowly.

“So the works of the flesh are not just personal sins.”

“No,” Elías said.

“They destroy relationships.”

Adrián leaned forward again.

“So let me see if I understand something.”

“What,” Marcos said.

“If jealousy and strife are works of the flesh…”

He paused.

“And factions are works of the flesh…”

He looked around the table.

“Then a church could be very active…”

“Yes.”

“Very organized…”

“Yes.”

“Very serious…”

“Yes.”

“And still be operating in the flesh.”

Matías didn’t answer.

He was still staring at the page.

Finally he said quietly,

“That’s disturbing.”

Adrián nodded.

“It is.”

Marcos spoke next.

“But it also explains something.”

“What.”

“Why churches that look strong from the outside sometimes feel unhealthy inside.”

Elías nodded slowly.

“Yes.”

“Because the flesh doesn’t always appear immoral.”

“No,” Marcos said.

“Sometimes it appears structured.”

Adrián laughed softly.

“That’s even worse.”

Matías finally looked up.

“There’s something else.”

“What.”

Matías pointed at the passage.

“Paul says the works of the flesh are *evident*.”

“Yes.”

“What do you think he means by that?”

Marcos answered first.

“That you can see them.”

“Yes.”

“In relationships.”

Adrián leaned forward again.

“So the flesh reveals itself.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“How?”

“By what it produces.”

The four men sat quietly for a moment.

Then Adrián spoke again.

“So if we followed this idea…”

He paused.

“We might be able to diagnose a church.”

Marcos nodded slowly.

“How.”

“By its fruit.”

Matías looked thoughtful.

“If jealousy, strife, and factions appear…”

He finished the thought quietly.

“Then the flesh is present.”

Elías closed the Bible gently.

“That seems to be what Paul is saying.”

Adrián looked around the table again.

“Well.”

He took a sip of his coffee.

“This investigation just became a lot more serious.”

Marcos nodded.

“Yes.”

Matías said nothing.

Because something about this passage had begun to bother him more deeply than the others realized.

If factions were works of the flesh…

Then some of the things he had always defended might not be as spiritual as he had believed.

And that thought was harder to ignore than he expected.
