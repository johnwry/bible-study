# Chapter 15

## What Do We Mean by “Flesh”?

For a while no one spoke.

Four churches.

Four very different pictures.

Corinth.

Galatia.

Ephesus.

Laodicea.

Each one different.

Yet each one corrected.

Adrián finally broke the silence.

“So now what?”

Marcos looked at him.

“What do you mean?”

“I mean we've been talking about these churches like we understand the problem.”

Matías nodded.

“Carnality.”

“Yes,” Adrián said.

“That word keeps coming up.”

Elías folded his hands on the table.

“It does.”

Adrián leaned forward slightly.

“But I’m starting to realize something.”

“What?” Marcos asked.

Adrián shrugged.

“I’m not sure I actually know what it means.”

The others looked at him.

Matías spoke first.

“You mean *flesh*?”

“Yes.”

Adrián nodded.

“We keep saying Corinth was fleshly.”

“And Galatia.”

“And possibly Ephesus.”

“And Laodicea.”

He paused.

“But what exactly are we saying?”

Marcos leaned back.

“That’s a fair question.”

Matías answered quickly.

“It’s sin.”

Adrián looked at him.

“Obvious sin?”

“Yes.”

“Immorality.”

“Drunkenness.”

“Those kinds of things.”

Adrián nodded slowly.

“That would explain Corinth.”

“Yes,” Matías said.

“But not the others.”

Marcos spoke.

“Galatia wasn’t immoral.”

“No,” Matías admitted.

“They were serious about obedience.”

Elías added quietly,

“Very serious.”

Adrián rubbed his chin.

“And Ephesus was doctrinally careful.”

“Yes.”

“And Laodicea was confident and successful.”

The table grew quiet again.

Finally Marcos said what all of them were thinking.

“So carnality must mean something more than obvious sin.”

Matías frowned slightly.

“What else could it mean?”

Elías spoke slowly.

“The word Paul uses in 1 Corinthians is interesting.”

Marcos looked up.

“Which word?”

“*Sarkikos.*”

Adrián smiled faintly.

“Here we go.”

Elías continued calmly.

“It literally means *of the flesh*.”

Matías nodded.

“That doesn’t clarify much.”

“No,” Elías admitted.

“But the context might.”

Marcos opened the Bible.

“Let’s look at it.”

He turned to **1 Corinthians 3**.

Adrián read aloud.

> “And I, brethren, could not speak to you as to spiritual men, but as to men of flesh…”

He paused.

“That’s the passage.”

Marcos nodded.

“Yes.”

Adrián continued.

> “As to infants in Christ.”

Matías frowned.

“They were immature.”

“Yes.”

Adrián read further.

> “For since there is jealousy and strife among you, are you not fleshly?”

He stopped.

“That seems straightforward.”

Marcos nodded.

“Jealousy and strife.”

“That’s the evidence.”

Matías leaned forward.

“So flesh means sinful behavior.”

“Possibly,” Marcos said.

“But the passage doesn’t stop there.”

Adrián looked down again and continued reading.

> “For when one says, ‘I am of Paul,’ and another, ‘I am of Apollos’…”

He paused.

“…are you not merely men?”

The table was quiet.

Matías spoke slowly.

“That sounds like pride.”

“Yes.”

“Division.”

“Yes.”

Adrián leaned back.

“So flesh shows up as jealousy…”

“…strife…”

“…division.”

Marcos nodded.

“Yes.”

But Elías was still looking at the passage.

“There’s something else here.”

“What,” Adrián asked.

Elías pointed to the text.

“They were still called *brothers*.”

Matías nodded.

“Yes.”

“And Paul still called them *infants in Christ*.”

Marcos leaned forward.

“So they were believers.”

“Yes.”

“But still fleshly.”

Adrián thought about that.

“That’s uncomfortable.”

“Why?” Matías asked.

“Because we tend to separate those two things.”

“What do you mean?”

“We assume flesh equals unbeliever.”

Elías shook his head slightly.

“Paul didn’t say that.”

Matías looked thoughtful.

“So believers can live according to the flesh.”

“Yes,” Marcos said.

Adrián tapped the table lightly.

“Okay.”

“So flesh is more than just immoral behavior.”

“Yes.”

“And it’s possible even in a church full of believers.”

“Yes.”

“But that still leaves the question.”

“What question?” Matías asked.

Adrián looked around the table.

“What exactly is the flesh?”

No one answered immediately.

Because each of them realized something.

They had used the word many times.

But none of them had ever stopped to carefully define it from Scripture.

Marcos finally spoke.

“If we’re going to continue this investigation…”

He looked at the others.

“…we probably need to answer that question.”

Elías nodded slowly.

“Yes.”

Matías folded his arms.

“So where do we start?”

Marcos answered simply.

“With the passages.”

Adrián smiled.

“That sounds like a long evening.”

Marcos nodded.

“It might be.”

Adrián leaned back.

“Well…”

He looked at the Bible again.

“…I guess we finally have our real question.”

The others looked at him.

Adrián said it plainly.

“If carnality can look like Corinth…”

“…or Galatia…”

“…or Ephesus…”

“…or Laodicea…”

He paused.

“…then what exactly does the Bible mean when it says *flesh*?”

And with that question, the investigation finally turned where it should have started all along.

To the Scriptures themselves.
