# Chapter 5

## Religious Flesh

No one spoke for a while.

The noise of the café carried on around them like nothing unusual had happened. Someone at the next table was explaining a travel story with wide gestures. A spoon clinked against a cup somewhere behind them.

But at the table by the window the four men sat quietly.

Adrián broke it first.

“Well.”

He looked around the table.

“That was not the direction I expected this to go.”

Marcos nodded.

“Same.”

Matías leaned forward slightly.

“I’m not convinced yet.”

Adrián smiled.

“I was wondering when that was coming.”

Matías ignored him.

“Just because the word appears in different contexts doesn’t mean we understand it.”

“That’s true,” Elías said.

“We need to be careful.”

Marcos looked at Matías.

“What part bothers you?”

Matías didn’t answer immediately.

Finally he said,

“This idea that the flesh could appear religious.”

Adrián leaned forward.

“You don’t think it can?”

“I’m not sure.”

Elías spoke quietly.

“But that’s exactly what Galatians sounds like.”

Matías looked at him.

“How.”

Elías opened the Bible again and turned a few pages.

“Look at the situation.”

The others leaned closer.

“These believers had begun with the Spirit.”

“Yes,” Marcos said.

“That’s what Paul said.”

“And now,” Elías continued, “they were trying to be perfected by the flesh.”

Matías nodded slowly.

“Yes.”

“But what were they actually doing?”

Adrián shrugged.

“Trying to obey God?”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“They were trying to keep the law.”

Marcos looked thoughtful.

“So they weren’t becoming immoral.”

“No.”

“They were becoming more serious.”

“Yes.”

Adrián leaned back.

“That’s interesting.”

Matías folded his arms.

“Trying to obey God is not a problem.”

“No,” Elías said.

“But Paul calls it flesh.”

The table went quiet again.

Adrián finally spoke.

“So let me see if I understand this.”

He looked at the open Bible.

“These believers started by trusting God.”

“Yes.”

“And then later…”

He gestured slightly.

“They started relying on their own effort.”

“That seems to be the problem,” Marcos said.

Matías frowned.

“But effort is part of discipline.”

“Yes,” Marcos said.

“But Paul isn’t criticizing discipline.”

“What then?”

“Dependence.”

Elías nodded.

“That’s the difference.”

Adrián pointed at the Bible.

“So the problem wasn’t that they wanted to obey God.”

“No.”

“The problem was how they were trying to do it.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

Matías was quiet.

Finally he spoke.

“That’s a dangerous idea.”

Adrián raised an eyebrow.

“Why.”

Matías answered slowly.

“Because if you tell people their effort is flesh…”

He paused.

“They might stop trying.”

Adrián laughed softly.

“I’ve heard that argument before.”

Marcos nodded.

“Most of us have.”

Elías spoke carefully.

“But that doesn’t change what the text says.”

Matías didn’t answer.

He was staring at the page.

Adrián leaned forward again.

“Let me ask something.”

“What,” Marcos said.

“If the Galatians were becoming more serious about religion…”

He paused.

“And Paul still calls it flesh…”

He looked around the table.

“Then what would that look like in a church today?”

No one answered right away.

Finally Marcos said,

“It might look very impressive.”

“How.”

“Very disciplined.”

“Very committed,” Elías added.

Matías spoke quietly.

“Very careful.”

Adrián nodded slowly.

“But underneath it…”

He pointed toward the Bible again.

“It would still be the flesh.”

The word hung in the air.

Matías finally looked up.

“That’s difficult to accept.”

“Why,” Adrián asked.

Matías took a long breath before answering.

“Because if that’s true…”

He paused.

“Then a church could look extremely serious about God…”

He looked back down at the page.

“And still be operating in the flesh.”

No one said anything.

Because they were beginning to realize something.

If that was true…

Then recognizing carnality might be much harder than any of them expected.
