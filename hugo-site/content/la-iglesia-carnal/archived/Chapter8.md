# Chapter 8

## Diagnosing a Church

The Bible was closed now.

Not because they were finished with it.

But because none of them were quite sure what to say next.

Adrián finally broke the silence.

“So if fruit reveals the source…”

He looked around the table.

“Then a church should be diagnosable.”

Marcos nodded.

“That seems logical.”

Elías leaned back slightly.

“Like a physician.”

“What do you mean?” Adrián asked.

“A doctor doesn’t guess about a disease.”

“No.”

“He looks at symptoms.”

Matías nodded slowly.

“And the symptoms reveal the condition.”

“Yes.”

Adrián smiled.

“Well then.”

He spread his hands slightly.

“What would the symptoms of a carnal church be?”

Marcos answered first.

“Jealousy.”

“Strife,” Elías added.

“Factions,” Adrián said.

Matías was quiet.

Those words had begun to feel uncomfortably familiar.

Marcos spoke again.

“You know something strange.”

“What,” Adrián said.

“The church I visited last week…”

He paused.

“I saw some of that.”

Adrián looked at him.

“What do you mean?”

Marcos shrugged slightly.

“Nothing dramatic.”

“But?”

“There were small groups forming.”

“Groups?”

“Yes.”

“Different camps.”

Elías nodded slowly.

“That sounds familiar.”

“Does it,” Adrián said.

“Yes.”

“Where?”

Elías leaned forward slightly.

“In Corinth.”

The table went quiet again.

Adrián smiled.

“You think that church was like Corinth?”

“I’m not saying that,” Elías said.

“I’m saying the fruit sounds similar.”

Matías spoke quietly.

“That’s dangerous.”

“Why?”

“Because once you start diagnosing churches…”

He paused.

“You might discover things you don’t want to discover.”

Adrián laughed softly.

“I think we already did.”

Marcos leaned forward again.

“Actually…”

He looked around the table.

“This might explain something else.”

“What,” Adrián said.

“The church I visited looked very healthy.”

“How.”

“Large.”

“Active.”

“Lots of ministries.”

“That sounds good,” Adrián said.

“Yes.”

“But underneath it…”

He hesitated.

“There was tension.”

Elías nodded slowly.

“Corinth looked impressive too.”

“That’s true,” Adrián said.

“They had gifts.”

“Yes,” Elías said.

“Many gifts.”

Matías spoke quietly.

“And yet Paul called them carnal.”

“Yes.”

Adrián leaned back.

“That’s uncomfortable.”

“Why,” Marcos asked.

“Because it means a church can look impressive…”

He pointed at the table.

“And still be operating in the flesh.”

Matías looked down again.

“That’s exactly what Paul said.”

Elías spoke carefully.

“So maybe we should do something.”

“What.”

Marcos answered.

“We should compare.”

“Compare what?” Adrián asked.

“The churches we visited.”

“With what?”

“With the churches in Scripture.”

Adrián smiled slowly.

“Now that…”

He lifted his cup again.

“That sounds like an investigation.”

Matías looked uneasy.

“But we need to be careful.”

“Why,” Marcos asked.

“Because diagnosing churches is not a small thing.”

“No,” Elías said.

“But ignoring Scripture would be worse.”

No one disagreed.

Adrián finally spoke.

“Well.”

He looked around the table.

“Let’s start with the obvious one.”

“What,” Marcos asked.

Adrián smiled.

“The church that reminded you of Corinth.”

Marcos shook his head.

“Not yet.”

“Why not?”

“Because I think you visited one first.”

Adrián laughed.

“That’s true.”

“And you said it felt…”

Marcos paused.

“Alive.”

Adrián nodded.

“Yes.”

“Well then,” Marcos said.

“Maybe we should start there.”

Adrián leaned back slowly.

“You want to hear about that church?”

“Yes.”

All three of them nodded.

Adrián took a slow sip of his coffee.

“Alright.”

He smiled slightly.

“Let me tell you what I saw.”
