# Chapter 9

## The Lively Church

Adrián leaned back in his chair and folded his arms.

“You’re going to regret asking me to describe it.”

Marcos smiled.

“Why.”

“Because I liked it.”

“That’s allowed.”

Adrián shook his head.

“Not if this turns into a theological interrogation.”

Elías smiled slightly.

“Just tell us what you saw.”

Adrián nodded.

“Alright.”

He leaned forward.

“So I arrived about ten minutes before the service started.”

“Large building?” Marcos asked.

“Yes.”

“Very.”

“Full?”

“Almost.”

Adrián laughed softly.

“Actually it filled up fast.”

He continued.

“People were greeting each other everywhere. Hugging. Laughing. Kids running around.”

“That sounds healthy,” Marcos said.

“It did.”

Matías nodded slightly.

“And the service?”

Adrián smiled.

“It started loud.”

“Loud?”

“Yes.”

“The music.”

Marcos nodded.

“Band?”

“Yes.”

“Good band?”

“Very.”

Adrián laughed.

“Actually impressive.”

He continued describing it.

“People were singing with their hands raised. Some had their eyes closed. Others were clapping.”

He shrugged.

“You could tell they meant it.”

Elías listened quietly.

“And the atmosphere?”

Adrián thought for a moment.

“Warm.”

“Very warm.”

“People seemed genuinely happy to be there.”

“That’s good,” Marcos said.

“Yes.”

Adrián nodded.

“Then something interesting happened.”

“What.”

“People began sharing things.”

“What kind of things?” Elías asked.

“Scripture.”

“Encouragement.”

“Prayers.”

“Someone even stood up and said they felt God had given them something to share.”

Matías raised an eyebrow.

“And how did the church respond?”

“They listened.”

“No chaos?”

“No.”

“It felt… free.”

Adrián paused for a moment.

“There was also food afterward.”

Marcos smiled.

“That always helps.”

Adrián laughed.

“Yes.”

“Tables everywhere.”

“People talking.”

“Kids playing.”

“It felt like a family gathering.”

Elías asked quietly,

“And the teaching?”

Adrián nodded.

“There was a short message.”

“Short?”

“Yes.”

“But it was good.”

“How.”

“Encouraging.”

“In what sense?”

Adrián shrugged.

“The speaker talked about trusting God.”

“Living by faith.”

“Depending on the Spirit.”

“That sounds biblical,” Matías said.

“Yes.”

“That’s why I liked it.”

Adrián leaned back again.

“I walked out of there thinking…”

He paused.

“That felt good.”

Marcos nodded slowly.

“And you would go back?”

“Absolutely.”

Elías spoke quietly.

“What made it feel alive to you?”

Adrián didn’t hesitate.

“The people.”

“They weren’t stiff.”

“They weren’t afraid.”

“They were engaged.”

Matías nodded slowly.

“That’s not a bad thing.”

“No,” Adrián said.

“It really wasn’t.”

For a moment the others sat quietly.

Marcos finally spoke.

“You know something.”

“What.”

“That description reminds me of something.”

Adrián smiled.

“What.”

Marcos looked at Elías.

Elías nodded slowly.

“Yes.”

Adrián looked between them.

“What are you two thinking?”

Elías reached for the Bible again.

“There was another church that sounded like that.”

“Which one?”

Elías turned a few pages.

“Corinth.”

Adrián laughed softly.

“Really?”

“Yes.”

Marcos nodded.

“They were lively too.”

Adrián leaned forward.

“Hold on.”

“What.”

“You’re not about to ruin my story, are you?”

Elías smiled faintly.

“Let’s just read it.”

Adrián shook his head with a grin.

“I knew this was coming.”

Matías leaned forward again.

“What does Paul say about Corinth?”

Elías opened the Bible.

“Quite a lot.”

Adrián lifted his cup again.

“Well.”

He smiled.

“Let’s hear it.”

------

This chapter does something very important:

It allows the reader to **like the church** first.

That makes the coming realization much stronger.

Next chapter will begin the **Corinth comparison**, where the men discover:

- Corinth had **many gifts**
- Corinth was **active**
- Corinth looked **alive**

Yet Paul still calls them **carnal**.
