# Chapter 1

## The Café

Adrián was already talking before anyone else had even arrived.

Standing at the counter.

One elbow on the wood, coffee in one hand, explaining something to the girl behind the register like they had known each other for years.

She was laughing while trying not to spill the coffee she was pouring.

“I’m serious,” Adrián said. “If you burn this one too I’m switching cafés.”

“You say that every week.”

“And yet here I am.”

She slid the cup across the counter.

Adrián lifted it, inspected it carefully, then nodded.

“Well look at that. You *can* learn.”

She rolled her eyes.

“You’re impossible.”

“That’s what my mother says.”

A man at a nearby table chuckled quietly.

“You talk to everyone like you’ve known them forever?”

Adrián turned toward him.

“No,” he said. “Some people I wait at least five minutes.”

The man laughed.

Adrián finally took a sip.

He closed his eyes for a second.

“Alright,” he said softly. “That’s better.”

Just then the bell above the café door rang.

Adrián looked up immediately.

“Elías!”

Elías stepped inside, closing the door behind him and pausing a moment while his eyes adjusted to the light in the room. He looked around the café the way some people do when they like to understand a place before sitting down in it.

When he saw Adrián he shook his head slightly.

“You’ve already made friends.”

“Of course.”

Adrián lifted his cup.

“You want coffee?”

“I’ll get my own.”

“Suit yourself. But this place is dangerous.”

Elías raised an eyebrow.

“Dangerous?”

“Yes.”

Adrián lowered his voice.

“If you come here too often you might start enjoying life.”

Elías allowed himself a small smile and walked to the counter.

A few minutes later he joined Adrián at the table by the window.

“You’re late,” Adrián said.

“I’m not late.”

“You are if I’m already on my second cup.”

“You’re always on your second cup.”

“That’s because life requires preparation.”

The bell rang again.

A man stepped inside carrying a small travel bag.

He paused just inside the door, scanning the room quickly before spotting them.

Adrián lifted his hand.

“There he is.”

Marcos nodded once and walked over.

“You two started without me?”

“We were discussing important matters,” Adrián said.

“Coffee?”

“No,” Adrián said. “Civilization.”

Marcos set his bag down.

“I’ll take coffee.”

“Good decision.”

Marcos went to the counter.

Adrián leaned back in his chair.

“You know what’s strange?” he said.

“What.”

“You can tell a lot about a person by how they order coffee.”

Elías looked at him.

“I’m sure you can.”

“If they add too much sugar they’re hiding something.”

“You just made that up.”

“Yes.”

“But it sounded convincing.”

“That’s a gift.”

Marcos returned with his coffee and sat down.

Before anyone said anything else, the bell above the door rang again.

Matías stepped inside.

He stood near the door for a moment, letting his eyes adjust before he spotted the table.

Adrián raised both hands.

“Finally.”

Matías walked over and set his bag down.

“You started already.”

“We’ve been here ten minutes.”

“You said two.”

“That was emotional time.”

Matías sat.

Now the four of them were there.

For a moment nobody spoke.

Not awkward.

Just the quiet that comes when people who know each other well sit down at the same table again.

Adrián finally broke it.

“You know what I noticed traveling last week?”

“That you talk too much?” Marcos said.

“That too.”

Adrián took another sip of coffee.

“But I visited a church while I was there.”

“That surprises no one,” Marcos said.

“It was good.”

“Good how?” Elías asked.

Adrián shrugged.

“I don’t know. Alive, I guess.”

“Alive,” Elías repeated.

“Yes.”

“That’s a technical term.”

“Very technical.”

Matías leaned forward slightly.

“What made it feel that way?”

Adrián thought for a moment.

“People.”

“In what sense?”

“They were… into it.”

“That doesn’t explain much.”

“I know.”

Adrián laughed.

“That’s the problem.”

Marcos stirred his coffee slowly.

“I visited one too.”

“Where?” Adrián asked.

“Different city.”

“And?”

Marcos shrugged.

“Also good.”

Elías raised an eyebrow.

“That clears everything up.”

Matías spoke next.

“I visited one as well.”

Adrián pointed at him.

“See? This is what I mean.”

“What.”

“We all travel. We all visit churches.”

“Yes.”

“And every time we come back saying the same thing.”

“What’s that?” Elías asked.

Adrián leaned forward slightly.

“I think it was good.”

Marcos smiled.

“That’s true.”

“But we never really explain why.”

“No,” Elías said.

They sat quietly for a moment while the sounds of the café moved around them. Cups clinked. Someone laughed near the door.

Adrián looked around the table.

“Let me ask something.”

Nobody answered yet.

“What actually makes a church healthy?”

Elías folded his hands.

“That depends what you mean by healthy.”

“That’s exactly my point.”

Matías nodded slowly.

“Scripture should define that.”

“Of course,” Adrián said.

“But if you asked ten people you’d get ten different answers.”

“That’s also true,” Marcos said.

Adrián stared into his coffee for a moment.

Then he looked back up.

“You know something else I’ve wondered?”

“What,” Marcos said.

Adrián looked at the three of them.

“What exactly is a *carnal* church?”

Nobody answered.

The word hung there for a moment.

Matías frowned slightly.

Elías looked down at the table.

Marcos tapped his spoon gently against his cup.

Finally Elías spoke.

“You know,” he said slowly,

“I distinctly remember Paul using that word somewhere.”

Adrián looked at him.

“Carnal?”

“Yes.”

“Where?”

Elías thought for a moment.

“Corinth, I think.”

Adrián turned his cup slowly in his hands.

“That’s interesting.”

Matías spoke quietly.

“Paul wasn’t talking about unbelievers.”

“No,” Elías said.

“He was talking about the church.”

Silence settled over the table.

Marcos finally spoke.

“If that’s true…”

He looked around at the others.

“Then maybe we’ve been asking the wrong question.”

Adrián tilted his head.

“What do you mean?”

Marcos shrugged slightly.

“Maybe the question isn’t what a good church looks like.”

He paused.

“Maybe the question is this…”

He looked at the others.

“What does Scripture say a **carnal church** actually is?”

No one answered.

But now they all wanted to know.
