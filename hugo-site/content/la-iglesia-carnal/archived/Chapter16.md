# Chapter 16

## Four Definitions

The question lingered longer than anyone expected.

What exactly is the flesh?

They had used the word easily.

Almost casually.

But once they tried to define it, the room became quieter.

Finally Adrián broke the silence.

“Well,” he said, “someone has to go first.”

Matías nodded.

“You started the question.”

“Which means I should start the trouble?”

Marcos smiled faintly.

“That seems fair.”

Adrián shrugged.

“Alright then.”

He leaned forward.

“To me it seems pretty simple.”

The others waited.

“The flesh is when people live according to their sinful desires.”

Matías nodded immediately.

“That makes sense.”

Adrián continued.

“You see it clearly in Corinth.”

“Pride.”

“Jealousy.”

“Immorality.”

“People doing whatever they want.”

He shrugged again.

“That’s the flesh.”

Matías nodded.

“Yes.”

“That fits.”

But Elías didn’t look fully convinced.

Adrián noticed.

“What.”

Elías spoke carefully.

“That definition works for Corinth.”

“Yes.”

“But it doesn’t explain Galatia.”

Adrián tilted his head.

“How not?”

“They weren’t chasing sinful desires.”

“They were trying to obey God.”

Adrián thought about that.

“Fair point.”

Matías leaned forward slightly.

“That’s exactly why I see it differently.”

Adrián smiled.

“Here we go.”

Matías spoke slowly.

“I think the flesh is when people stop taking holiness seriously.”

Adrián raised an eyebrow.

“Explain.”

“When discipline disappears.”

“When people stop guarding their lives.”

“When they assume grace will cover everything.”

Marcos looked thoughtful.

“So for you the flesh is lack of discipline.”

“Yes.”

Matías nodded firmly.

“Exactly.”

Adrián laughed softly.

“That sounds like half the churches I’ve ever visited.”

Matías didn’t laugh.

“Holiness matters.”

“No one disagrees with that,” Elías said.

Matías continued.

“When people begin relaxing their standards…”

“…the flesh takes over.”

Adrián leaned back.

“So the cure is stronger discipline.”

Matías shrugged.

“Obviously.”

Marcos looked toward Elías.

“You haven’t said anything yet.”

Elías spoke quietly.

“My concern is slightly different.”

Adrián smiled.

“I’m shocked.”

Elías ignored the comment.

“To me the flesh shows up when truth is compromised.”

Matías nodded slowly.

“That’s interesting.”

Elías continued.

“False ideas about God.”

“Careless teaching.”

“Doctrinal confusion.”

Adrián chuckled.

“That sounds exactly like you.”

Elías didn’t deny it.

“When truth becomes unclear…”

“…people begin drifting.”

Marcos asked,

“So the flesh, in your view, is doctrinal error?”

“In many cases, yes.”

Adrián rubbed his chin.

“That explains why you were so interested in Ephesus.”

“Yes.”

“They defended truth.”

“And yet Christ still corrected them,” Marcos said quietly.

Elías nodded slowly.

“Yes.”

The table grew quiet again.

Three definitions.

Sinful behavior.

Lack of discipline.

Doctrinal compromise.

Adrián finally looked at Marcos.

“You’ve been suspiciously quiet.”

Marcos smiled faintly.

“That’s because I’m not sure any of those explanations fully work.”

Matías frowned.

“What do you mean?”

Marcos leaned forward slightly.

“All three definitions describe real problems.”

“Yes.”

“But none of them explains every church we looked at.”

Adrián nodded.

“That’s true.”

Marcos continued.

“Corinth fits Adrián’s definition.”

“Yes.”

“Galatia fits Matías’ concern.”

“Yes.”

“And Ephesus relates to Elías’ warning.”

“Yes.”

“But Laodicea…”

He paused.

“…doesn’t fit neatly into any of them.”

Matías thought about that.

“They weren’t immoral.”

“No.”

“They weren’t careless.”

“No.”

“They weren’t doctrinally chaotic.”

“No.”

Adrián looked puzzled.

“So what was their problem?”

Marcos answered slowly.

“That’s exactly the question.”

Elías leaned forward slightly.

“So how would you define the flesh?”

Marcos hesitated.

“I’m not ready to define it yet.”

Adrián laughed.

“That’s convenient.”

Marcos smiled.

“I’m serious.”

“Why.”

“Because the passages we’ve read seem to point to something deeper.”

Matías crossed his arms.

“Deeper how.”

Marcos spoke carefully.

“In every case…”

“…the people involved believed they were doing the right thing.”

The table went quiet.

Adrián thought about it.

“Corinth thought they were spiritually gifted.”

“Yes.”

“Galatia thought they were protecting holiness.”

“Yes.”

“Ephesus thought they were defending truth.”

“Yes.”

“And Laodicea thought they were successful.”

“Yes.”

Marcos nodded slowly.

“And yet Christ corrected all of them.”

Elías looked thoughtful.

“So you think the flesh might be something more subtle.”

“Yes.”

“Something that can hide even inside sincere efforts to serve God.”

The words settled heavily on the table.

Matías finally spoke.

“That’s a troubling thought.”

Adrián nodded.

“Yes it is.”

Marcos leaned back slightly.

“I think we need to look at more passages.”

Elías nodded.

“Which ones.”

Marcos answered quietly.

“Romans.”

“Galatians.”

Adrián smiled faintly.

“Well then.”

He tapped the Bible lightly.

“Looks like this investigation is just getting started.”
